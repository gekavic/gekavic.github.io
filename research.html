Research Activity

I am a PhD researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto. My work focuses on modeling ion adsorption and transport at battery electrolyte interfaces, with an emphasis on multiscale modeling approaches that bridge atomistic simulations and continuum frameworks.


Research Focus

My research integrates molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo simulations, and continuum modeling to understand interfacial electrochemistry. I am particularly interested in how nanoscale structure and dynamics influence macroscopic transport properties, and how predictive models can accelerate the design of next-generation electrochemical systems.


Methods

  • Molecular Dynamics simulations of electrolytes and interfaces
  • Monte Carlo sampling for adsorption and equilibrium studies
  • Continuum and PDE-based modeling of ion transport
  • Multiscale frameworks linking atomistic and device-level behavior
