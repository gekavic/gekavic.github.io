Research Portfolio

A selection of my academic projects and research contributions in electrochemical systems and computational modeling.

Multiscale Modeling of Battery Interfaces

This project focuses on developing predictive models of ion adsorption and transport at electrolyte–electrode interfaces. Using molecular dynamics and Monte Carlo simulations, I investigate how interfacial structure influences charge transport and stability in next-generation batteries.

GitHub Repository

Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Electrolytes

Through atomistic simulations, I explore solvation structures, ion mobility, and interfacial organization in liquid electrolytes. These insights help bridge the gap between experimental electrochemistry and theoretical predictions.

Google Scholar

Monte Carlo Methods for Ion Adsorption

I apply Monte Carlo sampling techniques to study adsorption phenomena at charged surfaces. This work provides statistical insights into equilibrium configurations and supports continuum-scale modeling efforts.
